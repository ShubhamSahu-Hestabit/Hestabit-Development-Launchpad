## ğŸ§  Problem Statement

High-tension (HT) transmission and distribution lines use insulator assemblies
to electrically isolate live conductors. Birds such as pigeons and large raptors
often perch or nest near these insulators.

Bird droppings and nesting debris contaminate insulator surfaces, leading to:
- Increased leakage current
- Surface tracking
- Flashover risk
- Higher maintenance and reliability issues

This blog explains the **problem, impact, and a safe mitigation concept**.
